An unmissable stop ... Puerto NariÃ±o

A model of ecotourism, Puerto NariÃ±o is the second largest town in the Amazonas department. It is accessible by boat from Leticia, which itself is connected by plane to BogotÃ¡.

With a thriving and confident local culture, visitors can discover a wealth of indigenous traditions, unique biodiversity, and a way of life in harmony with the environment.

The "Amazonian summer" lasts about 6 months, from June to November, and marks a period of strong river recession. Several advantages: plenty of swimming with the appearance of beaches along the riverbanks, beautiful sunsets, and easier whale-watching.
Winter lasts for the rest of the year and brings torrential rains with the rainy season. The river level rises drastically (locally close to 10 meters), allowing you to admire the flooded forest landscapes and spot sloths along the way. This period is therefore more suitable for water activities.
